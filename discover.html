Our Stories

Java Justice began when a group of coffee lovers based in Atlanta, GA travelled to Colombia and toured some local farming communities. What began as a dream conversation soon became a partnership to bring affordable fair-trade, certified organic coffee to the masses while creating sustainable economic growth solutions for the local farms.

Our Mission

Our Mission is to stimulate economic growth in small coffee farming communities by funneling proceeds from the sale of fair trade certified organic coffee toward the development of stable infrastructure and increased cultural sustainability.

10,000 trees donated

Java Justice has donated more than 10,000 coffee trees over the past three years to farmers in the Huila region. These climate-resilient trees are replacing those that are declining in productivity due to age and disease, such as coffee leaf rust.

Java Justice supports programs around women’s leadership

These projects aim to break down barriers to education, promote clean water and sanitation, and create economic opportunities for women and girls.

02

january 2018

Support farmer profitability above commercial market price

Java Justice pays premiums that support farmer profitability above commercial market price. These premiums are driven by the fact we buy coffee that’s premium-quality and verified as ethically and sustainably sourced by C.A.F.E. Practice standards. In Chile, Java Justice has consistently paid a 5% premium above market prices.
